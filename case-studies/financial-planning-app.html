UX Design for Financial Planning App

In order to expand the reach and better one of their live events, the team behind an brand came for a technology partner. The event consists of a course that aims to improve people’s control over their financial lives. The product, a web application, works as a tool to help users understand their financial situation, and help them in the planning and tracking of their financial goals.

I was involved as a UX/UI Designer for this project, working from the Discovery phase onwards, until the implementation of the product.

see on behance

The challenge

The application had not been updated in some time, resulting in an outdated product that wasn’t truly supporting its users in the course as expected, and it didn’t follow the current branding. A specific problem was that around 70% of users weren’t completing the input of their basic financial information, which is fundamental to understanding their current economic situation, the event content and fulfilling the goals of the event.

Process

Heuristic Evaluation

In order to find general usability problems in the current solution we did a Heuristic Evaluation of the current tool. This helped us identify from a general point of view, where we identified usability problems and allowed us to take action in order to improve them.

Demographic Survey

A demographic survey helped us identify who our users were and was a great way to connect with them and start building a profile for our personas.

Qualitative Interviews

From the survey we were able to contact users for interviews, these really helped in understanding pain points and what users needed.

Personas

Based on our Interviews and Demographic Survey we developed a Persona at this stage in order to help us bring our knowledge of users and the problems they were facing to the whole team, also as a clear view of what we had to keep in mind when working on this project.

User Journey

Building a User Journey was a great way to align, understand and communicate both with stakeholders and the team. It allowed us to see the big picture and how it all came together, how our users were feeling and which were the exact stages of the experience we needed to improve.
